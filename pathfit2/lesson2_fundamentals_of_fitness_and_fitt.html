Fundamentals of Fitness

This lesson combines foundational training principles with the FITT principle framework to provide a complete guide for designing effective exercise programs.

Lesson Overview

Understanding the fundamental principles of fitness is crucial for developing an effective exercise program. These principles guide how we should structure our workouts, progress over time, and achieve optimal results safely and efficiently. The FITT Principle provides a practical framework for applying these fundamentals.

Core Principles of Fitness Training

Training Principles

  • Overload - Challenging the body beyond its normal level. To improve fitness, you must apply greater demands than the body is accustomed to.
  • Progression - Gradually increasing demands. As your body adapts, you must continue to increase intensity, duration, or frequency to continue making improvements.
  • Specificity - Training for specific goals. The adaptations your body makes are specific to the type of training you do.
  • Reversibility - Use it or lose it. Fitness gains are reversible if you stop training or reduce training significantly.
  • Individual Differences - Personal adaptation rates. People respond differently to training based on genetics, age, fitness level, and other factors.

Program Variables

  • Frequency - How often to train
  • Intensity - How hard to train
  • Time - Duration of training
  • Type - Kind of exercise
  • Volume - Total amount of work

The FITT Principle

The FITT Principle is a fundamental framework for designing effective exercise programs. It considers four key variables: Frequency, Intensity, Time, and Type. Understanding and applying these principles helps create structured and effective workout plans.

Frequency

How often you exercise, typically measured in sessions per week.

  • Cardiovascular training: 3-5 days per week
  • Strength training: 2-3 days per week (allowing for recovery)
  • Flexibility: Daily or at least 3-4 days per week
  • Beginners: Start with 2-3 days per week of any exercise

Intensity

How hard you work during exercise. Measured as percentage of max heart rate, perceived exertion, or weight lifted.

  • Light: 50-60% max heart rate or RPE 2-4
  • Moderate: 60-70% max heart rate or RPE 5-6
  • Vigorous: 70-85% max heart rate or RPE 7-8
  • For strength: 60-85% of one-rep maximum (1RM)

Time (Duration)

How long each exercise session lasts.

  • Cardiovascular: 20-60 minutes of continuous activity, or accumulate 10-minute bouts
  • Strength training: 30-60 minutes per session
  • Flexibility: 15-30 minutes
  • Include: 5-10 minute warm-up and 5-10 minute cool-down in total session time

Type (Mode)

The kind of exercise you do. Type should match your goals, preferences, and available resources.

  • Cardiovascular: Running, cycling, swimming, rowing, dancing
  • Strength: Free weights, machines, resistance bands, bodyweight exercises
  • Flexibility: Static stretching, yoga, dynamic stretching
  • Choose activities you enjoy to improve long-term adherence

Applying FITT Principles by Goal

General Guidelines

Cardiovascular Endurance

  • Frequency: 3-5 days/week
  • Intensity: 60-80% max heart rate
  • Time: 20-60 minutes
  • Type: Running, cycling, swimming, aerobic classes

Strength Training

  • Frequency: 2-3 days/week
  • Intensity: 65-85% 1RM
  • Time: 45-60 minutes
  • Type: Resistance exercises (weights, bands, bodyweight)

Flexibility

  • Frequency: Daily or 3-4 days/week minimum
  • Intensity: Mild tension (no pain)
  • Time: 15-30 minutes
  • Type: Static stretching, yoga, dynamic stretching

Safety and Proper Form

Essential considerations for safe and effective training:

  • Proper warm-up and cool-down
  • Correct exercise technique
  • Appropriate rest periods between exercises and sets
  • Injury prevention strategies
  • Recovery techniques and rest days

Key Takeaways

  1. Fitness progress relies on fundamental training principles (overload, progression, specificity, reversibility).
  2. FITT provides a practical framework for program design.
  3. Individual differences affect how people respond to training.
  4. Proper application of FITT variables allows customization for different goals.
  5. Safety, technique, and recovery are essential for long-term success.
