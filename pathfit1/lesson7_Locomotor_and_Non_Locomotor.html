Locomotor and Non-Locomotor Skills

Definition of Terms

Movement is the act of maintaining or changing the position(s) of body part(s). Some movements are simple (e.g. walking) while others are complex (e.g. turning a somersault).

Fundamental Movement (FM) is a movement that is simple and basic for participating in or performing different types of physical activities (e.g. walking, running, jumping, catching).

FM skills are refined fundamental movements for efficiency or expressive purposes. They need to be learned.

Physical Activity is defined as repetitive movements by the skeletal muscles that require energy and produce health benefits.

Physical Fitness is the body's capacity to adapt and respond favorably to physical effort. The physically fit person can move efficiently and effectively in meeting physical demands.

Locomotor skills are FM skills that we do when travelling from one place to another.

Stability Skills are FM skills for maintaining balance on spot or when moving.

Manipulative skills are FM skills for controlling small objects.

FM activities are activities which are conducive to the development of FM skills.

  • Manipulative – to catch the ball
  • Stability – to maintain balance when moving

Body Awareness

Awareness of one's body helps to understand its parts and identify possible movements for each segment. It recognizes the way the body or its parts can be controlled, moved and balanced.

Posture and Body Mechanics

Correct Posture - is the proper alignment of body segments in relation to different body positions.

Body Mechanics – exercises or movements intended to improve one's posture, stamina, and poise.
