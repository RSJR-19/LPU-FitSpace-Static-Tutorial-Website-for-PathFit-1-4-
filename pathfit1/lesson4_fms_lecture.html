Functional Movement Screen (FMS)

What is FMS?

The Functional Movement Screen (FMS) is a screening tool used to evaluate seven fundamental movement patterns in individuals with no current pain or musculoskeletal injury. It identifies opportunities for improving movement quality.

Screening Guidelines

  • Perform the test with assistance or use a mirror/camera for proper execution.
  • Three attempts are allowed for each test to observe movement from different angles.
  • Wear training shoes for consistent screening conditions.
  • Perform the screen with no prior warm-up or stretching to assess natural movement patterns.

FMS Test Patterns

1. Deep Squat

Challenges total body mechanics and neuromuscular control.

Instructions: Stand feet shoulder-width apart, hold a dowel overhead, squat deeply, hold, and return.

Scoring:

  • 3 – Torso parallel with tibia, femur below horizontal, knees aligned, dowel over feet.
  • 2 – Heels elevated or minor misalignment.
  • 1 – Significant misalignment.
  • 0 – Pain associated with movement.

Reference: Video Link

2. Hurdle Step

Tests step/stride mechanics, stability, and control in single-leg stance.

Instructions: Hold dowel, step over hurdle with proper alignment, return.

  • 3 – Perfect alignment, minimal lumbar movement, dowel/hurdle parallel.
  • 2 – Some alignment loss or lumbar movement.
  • 1 – Inability to clear hurdle or loss of balance.
  • 0 – Pain associated.

Reference: Video Link

3. Inline Lunge

Evaluates mobility, stability, and balance.

Instructions: Step on board, dowel along spine, lunge deeply maintaining posture, return.

  • 3 – Proper dowel contact, minimal torso movement, correct knee placement.
  • 2 – Loss of balance, partial completion.
  • 1 – Inability to complete movement.
  • 0 – Pain associated.

Reference: Video Link

4. Shoulder Mobility

Assesses bilateral shoulder mobility.

  • 3 – Fists within one hand length.
  • 2 – Fists within one and a half hand lengths.
  • 1 – Fists not within one and a half hand lengths.
  • 0 – Pain associated.

Reference: Video Link

5. Active Leg Raise

Evaluates active hamstring and core stability.

  • 3 – Vertical line between mid-thigh and ASIS, non-moving limb neutral.
  • 2 – Vertical line between mid-thigh and mid-patella.
  • 1 – Vertical line below mid-patella.
  • 0 – Pain associated.

Reference: Video Link

6. Trunk Stability Push-Up

Assesses core stability in push-up movement.

  • 3 – Proper alignment, body lifts as unit, thumbs aligned correctly.
  • 2 – Minor misalignment of thumbs or lift.
  • 1 – Significant misalignment.
  • 0 – Pain associated.

Reference: Video Link

7. Rotary Stability

Assesses multi-plane pelvis, core, and shoulder stability.

  • 3 – Correct unilateral repetition, limbs over board, elbow-knee touch maintained.
  • 2 – Correct diagonal repetition.
  • 1 – Inability to perform diagonal repetition.
  • 0 – Pain associated.

Reference: Video Link

Key Takeaways

  1. FMS evaluates seven fundamental movement patterns.
  2. Scores identify movement limitations and risk factors.
  3. Pain during tests requires medical evaluation.
  4. Screening helps guide exercise interventions and improvement.
