EATING HABITS

Why There is Eating Disorder?

  • Genetic: Some genes are associated with eating disorders and personality traits.
  • Biochemical: Abnormal chemical levels affecting appetite, mood, sleep, and stress.

Common Types of Eating Disorder

  • Anorexia Nervosa: Fear of gaining weight, severe food restriction, distorted body image.
  • Bulimia Nervosa: Binge eating followed by purging behaviors.
  • Binge Eating Disorder: Eating large amounts of food with loss of control.
  • ARFID: Avoidance due to sensory issues or lack of interest in food.

“Eating disorders are illnesses, not choices.” Individuals can be underweight, normal weight, or overweight. You cannot diagnose someone just by appearance.

Four Types of Eating You Should Know

  1. Fuel Eating: Eating to nourish the body; should be 90% of food intake.
  2. Joy Eating: Eating for pleasure.
  3. Fog Eating: Eating without awareness (e.g., snacking while distracted).
  4. Storm Eating: Eating when not hungry and feeling unable to stop.

Tips

  • Know your limitations
  • Eat fruits and vegetables
  • Control yourself
