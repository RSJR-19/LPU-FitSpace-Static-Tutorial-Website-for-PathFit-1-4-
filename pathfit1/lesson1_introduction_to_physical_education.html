Introduction to Physical Education

What is Physical Education?

Physical Education is part of the educational program designed to promote the optimum development of an individual through total body movement in the performance of properly selected physical activities.

Objectives of Physical Education

Physical Development

An individual who participates in properly selected physical activities will develop and maintain a high level of fitness.

Mental Development

The individual develops his mental expertise as he learns the mechanical principles and underlying movement, knowledge, and understanding of rules and strategies of games and sports.

Emotional Development

PE offers opportunities for self-expression and emotional mastery. Examples: self-confidence, self-reliance, determination, courage.

Social Development

Activities in PE provide opportunities to develop desirable social traits. Examples: friendliness, cooperation, respect, sportsmanship, leadership, honesty.

Legal Bases of Physical Education

International

  • 1978 - UNESCO: Physical education is a fundamental right for all.
  • 2003 - UN: Sport advances Millennium Development Goals.
  • 2005 - Resolution 58/5: International Year of Sport and Physical Education.

Philippines

  • 1987 Constitution, Article XIV, Section 19, mandates PE promotion, sports programs, and competitions.
  • All educational institutions must undertake regular sports activities nationwide.

Minimum Standards of Physical Education Program in College

PE 1 - PATHFit 1

Movement Competency Training: fundamental movement patterns integrated with core training, fitness concepts, exercise, and healthy eating principles.

PE 2 - PATHFit 2

Exercise-based Fitness Activities: enhance core stability, strength, mobility; includes goal setting, exercise principles, and periodic assessments.

PE 3 - PATHFit 3

Dance, Sports, Martial Arts, Group Exercise, Outdoor & Adventure Activities: optimize health, fitness, and skill proficiency; promote lifelong participation.

PE 4 - PATHFit 4

Same as PE3 – promotes health, fitness, skill proficiency, and lifelong participation.

Key Takeaways

  1. Physical Education develops the whole person through movement.
  2. It improves physical, mental, emotional, and social development.
  3. Legal bases support the importance of PE internationally and in the Philippines.
  4. Minimum standards ensure structured progression through PATHFit 1–4.
  5. Participation in PE fosters lifelong health and wellness.
