VOLLEYBALL

Lesson Overview

Volleyball is a dynamic team sport that develops agility, coordination, endurance, and teamwork. This course introduces the fundamental skills, rules, and strategies essential for playing volleyball effectively.

Through drills, gameplay, and performance assessments, students will enhance their motor skills, tactical understanding, and overall fitness while participating in structured volleyball activities.

What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is a team sport played by two teams on a rectangular court divided by a net. The objective is to send the ball over the net and ground it on the opponent’s side while preventing the opponent from doing the same.

The sport emphasizes communication, teamwork, and quick reflexes, making it highly popular in schools, recreational leagues, and professional competitions worldwide.

Nature and Background

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in Holyoke, Massachusetts, as a combination of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball to create a less strenuous sport.

The sport gained rapid popularity, leading to the establishment of formal rules, competitive leagues, and international competitions. Today, volleyball is played both indoors and on sand (beach volleyball), with worldwide recognition and professional tournaments.

Basic Skills in Volleyball

Serving

The serve is used to start a rally and can be performed underhand or overhand.

Passing / Bumping

Passing is used to control the ball after receiving a serve or attack and set it up for a teammate.

Setting

Setting involves using the fingers to accurately place the ball for an attacker to spike.

Attacking / Spiking

Spiking is a forceful overhead hit intended to ground the ball on the opponent’s court.

Blocking

Blocking is a defensive action where players jump near the net to prevent the opponent’s attack from crossing the net.

Volleyball Court and Equipment

1. Volleyball Court

The court is rectangular, measuring 18m by 9m, divided by a net. Lines mark attack zones, service areas, and boundaries.

2. Volleyball

The ball is spherical, lightweight, and designed for grip and control. Size and weight may vary for indoor or beach volleyball.

3. Net

The net divides the court and varies in height depending on age and gender: 2.43m for men, 2.24m for women, and 2.24m for coed youth leagues.

Volleyball Rules and Regulations

Volleyball has specific rules to ensure fair and competitive play:

  • A match is played best of five sets, each set to 25 points (15 points for the deciding set)

  • A team must win a set by at least 2 points

  • Each team has six players on the court at a time

  • Players are not allowed to touch the net or cross the centerline

  • Each team can hit the ball up to three times before sending it over the net

Governing Body in Volleyball

  • The Fédération Internationale de Volleyball (FIVB) governs international volleyball competitions and rules.

  • The Philippine National Volleyball Federation (PNVF) governs volleyball locally and is recognized by the FIVB and Olympic Committee.

Volleyball Officials

There are several key officials in volleyball:

1. Referee

The Referee supervises the entire match, enforces rules, and ensures fair play.

2. Second Referee

The Second Referee assists the first referee, monitors substitutions, and manages the net area.

3. Line Judges

Line Judges observe whether the ball lands in or out of the court and help the referees make accurate calls.

Key Takeaways

  1. Volleyball is a high-energy team sport that enhances physical fitness, agility, and coordination.
  2. Mastering fundamental skills such as serving, passing, setting, spiking, and blocking is essential for effective gameplay.
  3. Understanding rules, court layout, and player rotations ensures fair and competitive matches.
  4. Regular participation improves teamwork, reflexes, strategic thinking, and mental alertness.
  5. The sport is governed internationally by FIVB and locally by PNVF to maintain standardized rules and development.
