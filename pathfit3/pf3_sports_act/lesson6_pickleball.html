PICKLEBALL

Lesson Overview

Pickleball is a paddle sport that combines elements of tennis, badminton, and table tennis. This course introduces the fundamentals of pickleball, including basic skills, rules, and the physical benefits gained from playing.

Through drills, gameplay, and performance assessments, students will improve hand-eye coordination, agility, endurance, and teamwork while participating in structured pickleball activities.

What is Pickleball?

Pickleball is played on a court similar in size to a badminton court, using paddles and a perforated polymer ball. It can be played as singles or doubles, and the objective is to send the ball over the net and land it in the opponent’s court.

The sport emphasizes quick reflexes, strategic placement, and teamwork, making it accessible for all ages and skill levels.

Nature and Background

Pickleball was invented in 1965 by Joel Pritchard, Bill Bell, and Barney McCallum in Bainbridge Island, Washington, as a family-friendly sport combining elements of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong.

Since its creation, pickleball has grown rapidly in popularity, with formal rules, organized leagues, and international competitions. It is recognized as one of the fastest-growing sports in the United States and worldwide.

Basic Skills in Pickleball

Serving

The serve is made underhand from behind the baseline and must land in the diagonal service court.

Dinking

Dinking is a soft shot near the net designed to force the opponent into a difficult position.

Groundstrokes

Groundstrokes are forehand or backhand shots hit after the ball bounces, used to maintain rallies or attack the opponent.

Volleying

Volleying is hitting the ball in the air before it bounces, usually near the net, to gain an offensive advantage.

Smashing / Overhead Shots

Smashes are powerful overhead shots used to finish a point decisively.

Pickleball Court and Equipment

1. Court

The court measures 20 by 44 feet for both singles and doubles, with a non-volley zone (“kitchen”) near the net to prevent smashes too close to the net.

2. Paddle

Paddles are solid and lightweight, made from wood, composite, or graphite materials, designed for quick reactions and ball control.

Pickleball Rules and Regulations

Pickleball has clear rules to maintain fairness and competition:

  • The serve must be underhand and land in the diagonal service court

  • Each side may hit the ball up to two times before returning it over the net

  • Players cannot step into the non-volley zone (kitchen) to hit a volley

  • Games are usually played to 11, 15, or 21 points, and a team must win by 2 points

  • Pickleball can be played as singles or doubles

Governing Body in Pickleball

  • The USA Pickleball Association (USAPA) oversees rules, rankings, and development in the United States.

  • The International Federation of Pickleball (IFP) governs international competitions and promotes global growth of the sport.

Pickleball Officials

Officials ensure the game is played fairly:

1. Referee

The Referee supervises the match, enforces rules, and resolves disputes.

2. Line Judges

Line Judges observe if the ball lands in or out and assist in making correct calls.

3. Scorekeeper

The Scorekeeper maintains the score and announces points accurately during the match.

Key Takeaways

  1. Pickleball is a fun and fast-growing paddle sport that develops hand-eye coordination, agility, and endurance.
  2. Mastering basic skills like serving, dinking, volleying, and smashing is essential for effective gameplay.
  3. Understanding court layout, rules, and the non-volley zone ensures fair and competitive matches.
  4. Regular participation improves teamwork, reflexes, strategic thinking, and overall fitness.
  5. The sport is governed internationally by the IFP and nationally by the USAPA to maintain standardized rules and development.
